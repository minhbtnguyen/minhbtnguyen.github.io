entries:
  - source: "Andrej Karpathy — micrograd"
    sourceUrl: "https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VMj-3S1tku0"
    repoUrl: "https://github.com/minhbtnguyen/micrograd"
    date: "2026-05"
    takeaway: "Autograd is just a graph of local derivatives — every op stores how it transformed its inputs, and backprop walks the graph in reverse, multiplying as it goes. The whole abstraction collapses to two methods: `__add__` and `__mul__` recording parents, plus `backward()` traversing them topologically."
  - source: "Rich Sutton — The Bitter Lesson (2019)"
    sourceUrl: "http://www.incompleteideas.net/IncIdeas/BitterLesson.html"
    date: "2026-04"
    takeaway: "Methods that scale with compute (search + learning) consistently beat methods that bake in human cleverness. Every time researchers tried to encode domain knowledge into the model, general-purpose methods + more compute eventually overtook them. Worth re-reading before designing any 'clever' architecture."
  - source: "Vaswani et al. — Attention Is All You Need (2017)"
    sourceUrl: "https://arxiv.org/abs/1706.03762"
    date: "2026-03"
    takeaway: "Self-attention is a soft, content-addressable lookup: each token computes a query, asks every other token for its key/value, and aggregates the values weighted by query·key similarity. The architectural insight isn't attention itself — it's that you can stack it without recurrence and still get sequence modeling, which unlocks parallelism on GPUs."
  - source: "Andrej Karpathy — nanoGPT"
    sourceUrl: "https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kCc8FmEb1nY"
    repoUrl: "https://github.com/minhbtnguyen/nanogpt"
    date: "2026-02"
    takeaway: "A transformer LM is mechanically simple: token + position embedding → N blocks of (multi-head self-attention + MLP, with residuals and layer norm) → linear head. Everything else — tokenization, optimizer choices, learning-rate schedule — is engineering that compounds, not theory."
